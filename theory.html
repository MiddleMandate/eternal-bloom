Theory Overview

Physics has a split-personality problem. Quantum mechanics says nature is fundamentally uncertain: particles exist in ghostly superpositions of many states at once, and definite outcomes only appear when we look. General relativity says spacetime is smooth, definite, and curved by mass and energy. Both theories work brilliantly in their own domains. But they disagree about what reality is, and nobody has been able to reconcile them.

The Unified Field Theory of Eternal Bloom (UFT-EB) proposes a resolution built on a single idea: classical reality emerges when the internal informational coherence of a quantum system crosses a critical threshold.

The Measurement Problem

When does a quantum system stop being "both here and there" and commit to one definite state? This is the measurement problem, and it has haunted physics since the 1920s. The standard answer is that an observer or measurement device causes the transition, but no one has ever specified exactly what counts as a "measurement" or why it should have such dramatic effects.

Various theories have tried to fix this. The GRW and CSL models say collapse happens randomly, governed by a universal noise field. Penrose and Diósi say gravity itself forces collapse when mass configurations diverge too much. The Many-Worlds interpretation says collapse never happens at all. Each captures something important, but none provides a complete, testable, observer-independent account of when and why the quantum world becomes the classical one.

The Bloom Mechanism

UFT-EB takes a different approach. The full informational evolution of a quantum system is governed by the root equation, which integrates local coherence measures across space:

Root Equation — Informational Evolution
$$\Omega(\tau) = \Psi_0 + \int \mathcal{I}(\nabla\Psi(x,\tau)) \cdot \Phi(x,\tau) \cdot e^{-\Xi(x,\tau)} \, d^3x$$

The key quantity within this integral is $\Phi$, a precise, calculable measure of a system's informational coherence. $\Phi$ combines three ingredients:

Entropy $S$

How much uncertainty or disorder exists in the system's quantum state. Computed via the von Neumann entropy of the density matrix.

Mutual Information $I$

How much shared quantum information exists between different parts of the system. This measures entanglement strength.

Decoherence $\Xi$

How much the environment has degraded the system's quantum coherence. Derived from Lindblad master equations and noise spectroscopy.

When $\Phi$ crosses the critical threshold $\Phi_c$, the system collapses from quantum superposition to classical definiteness. The trigger condition is deterministic: given complete knowledge of the density matrix, entropy, mutual information, and decoherence potential, the collapse point is calculable. The collapse dynamics, however, include a stochastic component—a light-cone-localized noise coupling that ensures Born-rule probabilities emerge correctly while preserving relativistic causality. This distinguishes UFT-EB from purely stochastic models (GRW, CSL) where both the trigger and the process are random, and from Copenhagen where the trigger is observer-dependent.

We call each collapse event a "bloom" because it represents the flowering of classical definiteness from quantum possibility. The universe is in a state of perpetual bloom.

The Bloom Field Equation

The spatial structure of informational coherence is governed by the Bloom Field Equation (BFE), derived from a variational principle applied to an informational Lagrangian:

Bloom Field Equation
$$\nabla^2 \Delta\Psi_i - \Gamma_{\text{futures}} \cdot \Theta = 0$$

This equation plays a role analogous to Einstein's field equations in general relativity, but for informational rather than gravitational fields. $\nabla^2\Delta\Psi_i$ measures the curvature of informational coherence in space (how rapidly coherence changes from one region to another). $\Gamma_{\text{futures}}$ propagates coherence forward in time. $\Theta$ is the informational energy density.

Where the BFE has non-zero residuals, informational equilibrium has been disrupted and collapse is imminent or gravitational anomalies may appear.

Making It Relativistic: The $\tau$-Lock

For the theory to be consistent with Einstein's relativity, collapse cannot happen instantaneously across space (that would allow faster-than-light communication). UFT-EB solves this with the $\tau$-lock formulation: a fully covariant version where the noise driving collapse is localized along the light cone. The collapse trigger is evaluated on spacelike hypersurfaces in the Tomonaga-Schwinger picture, and the noise kernel $K_\ell$ peaks at null separation.

This construction guarantees three essential properties: no superluminal signaling (collapse at one location cannot influence distant measurements faster than light), stress-energy conservation (energy is preserved in expectation), and Born-rule recovery (quantum probabilities emerge correctly from the formalism).

Gravity from Information

Perhaps the most striking feature of UFT-EB is its connection to gravity. Through the entropy-curvature coupling:

Modified Einstein Equations
$$G_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4}\left(\mathbb{E}[T_{\mu\nu}] + \lambda\,\Xi_{\mu\nu}[\varphi,\Phi]\right)$$

Standard general relativity says spacetime curvature ($G_{\mu\nu}$) is caused by energy and momentum ($T_{\mu\nu}$). UFT-EB adds a second source: the informational content of quantum fields ($\Xi_{\mu\nu}$). In the classical limit where quantum coherence is negligible, $\Xi_{\mu\nu} \to 0$ and standard GR is recovered exactly. But in quantum regimes and at cosmological scales, the informational correction generates observable effects.

Testable Predictions

UFT-EB is designed to be falsifiable. It makes specific predictions testable with current technology:

Quantum Optics

Multi-photon entangled states should show a sharp, deterministic coherence transition at $\Phi_c$, distinguishable from CSL's gradual stochastic decay.

Bose-Einstein Condensates

BEC phase coherence should persist until the threshold, then collapse rapidly. This trajectory differs qualitatively from standard decoherence models.

Cosmological Signatures

Dark energy may be sub-threshold informational coherence. The Hubble tension may reflect epoch-dependent informational corrections to gravitational coupling.
