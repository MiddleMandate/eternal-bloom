Abstract

We present the Unified Field Theory of Eternal Bloom (UFT-EB), an informational threshold framework that unifies quantum decoherence, emergent time, and gravitational interaction through a single organizing principle: classical reality emerges when internal informational coherence crosses a critical, observer-independent threshold. The theory is built on a variational informational action principle yielding the Bloom Field Equation (BFE), which governs the spatial structure of coherence fields. We derive a fully covariant τ-lock collapse formulation with light-cone-localized stochastic coupling, demonstrate no-signaling in expectation, and establish stress-energy conservation. Computational validation using Qiskit quantum circuits and lattice simulations confirms the critical threshold and produces predictive anomaly maps for gravitational lensing deviations. We propose falsifiable experimental tests across quantum optical interferometry, Bose-Einstein condensates, superconducting qubit platforms, and cosmological observation.
