About the Project

The Researcher

Alexander T. Sherrod is an independent researcher exploring the intersection of quantum information theory, gravitational physics, and computational simulation. The Unified Field Theory of Eternal Bloom grew out of a sustained investigation into informational geometry and quantum coherence dynamics, beginning in 2024.

Alexander's approach combines rigorous mathematical derivation with computational validation and adversarial testing, leveraging advanced AI systems as collaborative tools while maintaining full intellectual ownership of the theoretical framework and its core insights.

AI Collaboration

UFT-EB was developed through an iterative process involving human insight and AI-assisted analysis. GPT-4 (OpenAI) and Claude (Anthropic) served as collaborative tools for mathematical consistency checking, literature review, simulation debugging, and adversarial stress testing of the theory's foundations.

This collaboration model represents a new approach to theoretical physics research: the core ideas, physical intuitions, and the foundational concept of informational threshold collapse originate entirely with Alexander Sherrod. The AI systems served as tireless interlocutors, catching mathematical errors, suggesting relevant literature, and simulating hostile peer review to strengthen the theory before formal publication.

The Name

"Eternal Bloom" reflects the theory's central metaphor: reality is a perpetual informational unfolding. Each quantum collapse event is a "bloom" of classical definiteness emerging from quantum possibility. This process has no beginning or end. It is the continuous informational metabolism of the universe itself, an eternal flowering of actuality from potentiality.

The name also carries a personal resonance: the conviction that understanding, like nature, is always in the process of opening up.
